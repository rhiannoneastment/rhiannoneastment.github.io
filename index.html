Ecotoxicology, Evolution, & Reproduction

Postdoctoral Research Associate · The University of Manchester · Manchester, UK

Temp: I am an early career researcher interested in understanding the evolution of complex traits. I'm particularly interested in using omic methods, such as phylogenetics and comparative genomics, to better understand the evolution of complex forms of reproduction, such as viviparity and mouthbrooding. I'm also interested in understanding how these traits are impacted by anthropogenic change, such as pharmaceutical pollution.

Research Themes

My work uses a combination of bioinformatics, field work, and laboratory techniques to better understand the evolution of reproduction, and how it is impacted by pollution.

Comparative genomics and convergent evolution

comparative genomics is a valuble tool for understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying diverse reproductivestrategies. My work aims to uncover the molecular mechanisms associated with the convergent evolution of complex parental care strategies, from mouthbrooding in African cichlids to viviparity in vertebrates.

Ecotoxicology

The impact of enviroinmental pollution on wildlife is an area of growing concern. My research aims to broaden the taxonomic scope of ecotoxicological research to better understand how environmental contaminants interact with diverse phenotypes.
